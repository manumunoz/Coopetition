Instructions Part 2

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

In this part of the study, you choose between two schemes that determine how your Bonus 2 is calculated.

Afterwards, you will be randomly matched with another participant, who also performed the encryption task in Part 1, and who chose the same scheme as you.

One of you will be randomly labeled “Player A” and the other one “Player B”.

You and the other player will perform the encryption task in one scored round.

If you choose Scheme 1:

Your and the other player’s Bonus 2 is determined as follows:

Both players earn X.X$ times

  • the total number of your correct words.
  • half of the sum of both players' correct words

If you choose Scheme 2:

Your and the other player’s Bonus 2 is determined as follows:

The player who correctly encrypted fewer words than the other player earns X.X$ times

  • the total number of his/her correct words.
The player who correctly encrypted more words than the other player earns X.X$ times
  • the total number of his/her correct words.
  • two times her/his points
In case of a tie, the computer will randomly choose one of the two players to be paid the latter and the other one to be paid the former.

Example Scheme 1:

Player A encrypted 10 words correctly and Player B encrypted 12 words correctly
Half of the sum of both players’ correct words is (10+12) / 2 = 11
Then Player A receives a total of X.X$ * (10 + 11) = Y$
Then Player B receives a total of X.X$ * (12 + 11) = Y$

Example Scheme 2:

Player A encrypted 10 words correctly and Player B encrypted 12 words correctly
Half of the sum of both players’ correct words is (10+12) / 2 = 11
Then Player A receives a total of X.X$ * 10 = Y$
Then Player B receives a total of X.X$ * (12 * 3) = Y$

At the end of the experiment, you will receive information about the number of words that you and the other player encrypted correctly.
